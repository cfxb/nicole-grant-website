Frequently asked

FAQ

What does the free consultation cover?

We talk about what's going on for you, what you're looking for, what you'd get out of therapy, the cost, and the options for working together. It's about 15 minutes, by phone, at no charge.

Who do you work with?

Children, teens, young adults, and adults. Online, the focus tends to be teens, young adults, and adults, though younger children are seen too.

What do you help with?

Anxiety, phobias (including emetophobia), OCD, and trauma.

How do I book the consultation?

Email nicolegrantrcc@gmail.com with your name and your general availability. Please don't include sensitive personal details in that first message. I usually reply within three days.

Are you registered?

Yes. I'm an RCC and a member of the BC Association of Clinical Counsellors (BCACC). My listing is at https://bcacc.ca/counsellors/nicole-grant/

What times are available?

Daytime, evening, and weekend appointments are available, including after-school times for younger clients. We can check whether our schedules line up in the consultation.

Do you offer in-person sessions?

No. This practice is online only, across BC.

How long are sessions?

50 minutes.

What are your fees?

Fees are discussed during the free consultation.

What is CVAP?

The Crime Victim Assistance Program, a Government of BC program that can help cover counselling and other costs for people affected by violent crime. I'm a CVAP-approved counsellor and don't charge a top-up.

Do you offer services in languages other than English?

No, English only.
