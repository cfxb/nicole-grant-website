What I offer

Services

I offer online counselling across BC for children, teens, young adults, and adults. Sessions are 50 minutes, by secure video. My online work tends to focus on teens, young adults, and adults; I do see younger children, though the online focus skews a bit older.

Fees are discussed during the free consultation. I am a Crime Victim Assistance Program (CVAP) approved counselor, and accept CVAP-funded patients, with no top-up. Many patients receive reimbursement through their extended health benefits or Employee Assistance Programs.

Who I work with

People most often come to me for:

  • Anxiety
  • Phobias, including emetophobia (the fear of vomiting)
  • OCD
  • Trauma

Emetophobia

Emetophobia is the fear of vomiting. It's more common than people expect, and it can take over a lot of life. Some people can't get on a plane, or struggle at school, think about it every day, or find sleep difficult. It often shows up alongside health anxiety or OCD, with checking, reassurance-seeking, or other rituals to manage the "what if I get sick" feeling.

This is a big part of my online work. The approach is gradual and led by you, the same step-by-step way described on the home page.

How I work

These are the main approaches I draw on. I'll only use what fits you, and you can say yes or no to any of it.

CBT (Cognitive Behavioural Therapy). The way we feel is often shaped by what we're thinking. If I think a dog coming towards me is going to bite me, I feel scared and I cross the street. If I think about it differently, I might feel calmer and stay where I am. A lot of our work is looking at the thoughts that show up, and how they affect how you feel and what you do.

ERP (Exposure and Response Prevention). A structured way of facing a fear in small, planned steps, while not doing the usual thing that keeps the fear going (like avoiding it or seeking reassurance). We build the steps together and go at your pace.

EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitisation and Reprocessing). A structured way of working through memories and experiences that still feel raw. You bring a memory to mind while following a moving point with your eyes, or using another rhythmic focus, in short sets. I use this with trauma, and with phobias, since a phobia often has a frightening past experience behind it.

DBT (Dialectical Behaviour Therapy). A practical set of skills for managing strong emotions and getting through hard moments without things getting worse.

Clinical hypnosis. Getting into a relaxed, focused state, a bit like driving a familiar route and arriving without remembering each turn. In that state, we work from a goal you've set, using visualisation to build a feeling, like confidence, you want more of.

I also draw, more briefly, on play therapy, sand tray, art therapy, mindfulness, and solution-focused work, particularly with younger clients.

A first session

In the first session we talk about what you're struggling with, and what you're hoping for from therapy.I'll give you some ideas of things we could do, and you can say yes or no to any of them. By the end you'll see that the work is very much led by you, with my ideas and suggestions in the mix.

With children and teens, I often start with a scaling activity. We draw it out together on a big piece of paper. I name some things going on in your life, and you place them on a scale for how distressing or okay they feel. It helps us see what's going well, so we can use that, and what you want to focus on.
