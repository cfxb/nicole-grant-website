Online counselling for anxiety and phobias

Anxiety and phobias can make ordinary life feel much harder than it should. If you're looking for a way through — gently, at a pace that feels right for you — I'd like to help.

I'm Nicole Grant, RCC, MEd. I offer online counselling across BC for children, teens, young adults, and adults. My focus is anxiety, phobias — including emetophobia, the fear of vomiting — OCD, and trauma.

Starting therapy can feel like a big step. That's completely understandable. In my experience, a lot of that worry tends to ease once you see what the work actually looks like: me getting to know you, and us working out together what would help. We go at your pace. I check in with you along the way. Nothing happens before you're ready.

How we work through a phobia

People often come to me worried because they don't want to face the thing they're scared of. That makes complete sense. But phobia work isn't about throwing yourself in at the deep end.

I use a picture sometimes. Say you're scared to jump off a diving board. We don't start at the diving board. We start by sitting on the bleachers — just watching, just being near the pool. When that feels okay, we move a little closer. We sit on the edge and put our feet in. Then the whole body, but no jumping yet. Then jumping from the edge. Then a little higher. Each step, repeated until it feels boring, until you feel ready for the next one.

I check in at every step before we move on.
