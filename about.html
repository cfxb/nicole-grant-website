About

About Nicole Grant

I offer online counselling across BC. Before I trained as a counsellor, I spent years in schools, first as a teacher, then as a resource teacher and case manager.

I didn't plan to become a counsellor. I was working with a student who had selective mutism, alongside a counsellor, and I started to notice the difference between the two roles. So much of resource work was about moving a child toward goals other people had set. The counselling work was about meeting the person where they were and guiding them, rather than directing them. That suited me better, so I switched my training over to counselling.

What keeps me in this work now is the connection. It's not so much the progress, even though I love seeing progress. I just feel like it's such a privilege to be a witness to someone's life.

How I work

I try not to make therapy clinical or pathologising. A lot of it is just me getting to know you, and creating a space where you can talk about yourself and what's going on in your life.

With kids and teens, I get on their level. That often means working through play, or art, or visualisation, rather than questions the whole time. It also means taking a genuine interest in what they're actually into, including the games they're playing and the words they're using.

When the work is going well, I notice bravery. Not just with the specific fear we're working on, but spreading into the rest of life. People start putting their hand up more in class, or starting conversations with people they wouldn't normally talk to. The courage tends to generalise.

What I bring

I spent years in the school system, and I understand how schools work. For families, that can matter, especially if a child is going through an assessment or needs a designation. I know what other supports exist, so it's not only me you're working with. I can help you navigate the system around the school as well.

I also think the work should be enjoyable. I like working with kids and teens, and I'd like our time together to feel like a real conversation, not an interview.
